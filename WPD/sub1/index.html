3 Dimesional Space

Basic History

The philosophy of space dates back to Isaac Newton in the 17th century. Newton believed that space is absolute. He this as an example: If the bucket's spinning is stopped then the surface of the water remains concave as it continues to spin. The concave surface is therefore apparently not the result of relative motion between the bucket and the water. Newton argued that the water is relative to space itself.

 

Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist in the early 20th century, who developed general and special relativity. He theorized that instead of percieving gravity as a force, it actually manipulates the geometric structure of spacetime itself. Einstein also predicted that gravity is able to slow time, another aspect called time-dilation.

General Information

Space makes up three of the 4 dimensions of our (known) universe: X, Y, and Z. You must think of these as on a 3-dimensional graph: the X-axis is horizontal, the Y-axis is vertical, and the Z-axis is forward and back. Space appears to us to be ever expansive.

Matter

Matter is, basically, that which occupies space and posses rest mass, as distinct from energy. There are many different kinds of matter, which we have organized into various categories and subcategories, each with their own preperties and characteristics.
